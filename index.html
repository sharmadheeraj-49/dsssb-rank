🧠 The Scientific Methodology

How do we predict your rank with mathematical precision? This is not a basic spreadsheet. We simulate the entire exam process using historical attrition rates and category shifting rules.

1. The 8.32x Shortlisting Factor

DSSSB originally released ~2,354 vacancies, later revised to ~3,050. They officially shortlisted 25,385 candidates for the typing test. This gives us a precise shortlisting multiplier of 8.32x for every category.

2. Asymmetric Attrition (The 60%ile Rule)

In competitive exams, the "Creamy Layer" (top 5-10% scorers) skip the typing test at a massive rate because they are often CGL/UPSC aspirants testing their speed. While average attendance is ~70% and typing pass rate is ~50%, the survival rate of the extreme top layer is much lower. Because the top layer vanishes, the selection depth reaches much further down the merit list. Mathematics proves that crossing the 60th Percentile in your true pool places you in the safe selection zone.

3. The Fallback Effect (Category Shifting)

If a reserved candidate (OBC/SC/ST/EWS) scores above the Final UR Cutoff and passes typing, they legally consume a General (UR) seat, not a reserved seat. Our algorithm physically purges these top-scorers from your category pool and recalculates your "True Percentile" based only on the remaining candidates fighting for the reserved seats. This is why you will see your category percentile jump significantly!
